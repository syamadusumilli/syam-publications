Rural Health Transformation Project Version 3: January 2026

Purpose#

This technical document serves as a master reference documenting who receives RHTP funding across all 50 states. The document enables:

  1. Cross-state comparison of intermediary structures and program types
  2. Identification of key organizations operating in the RHTP ecosystem
  3. Analysis of funding distribution patterns from state agencies to end recipients
  4. Tracking of implementation models across different state contexts

Understanding where money flows reveals more about RHTP implementation than headline award figures. States channel billions through intermediary organizations, and these distribution decisions shape what transformation actually looks like on the ground.

Section 3: Funding Distribution Patterns#

Intermediary Versus Direct Award Ratios#

States vary dramatically in how much funding flows through intermediary organizations versus direct awards to providers.

State Via Intermediaries Direct to Providers Administrative Distribution Model
California 40-50% 40-50% 10% Balanced
Texas 20-30% 60-70% 7-9% Provider-heavy
Arkansas 50-60% 30-40% ~3% Intermediary-heavy
Missouri 60-70% 20-30% ~5% Intermediary-heavy
Ohio 30-40% 50-60% ~10% Balanced
West Virginia 40-50% 40-50% ~5% Balanced
Wisconsin 30-40% 50-60% ~5% Provider-heavy

Pattern observed: States with established intermediary infrastructure (Missouri with ToRCH, Arkansas with UAMS/Arkansas Rural Health Partnership) channel more funding through these organizations. States relying on competitive procurement (Texas) direct more funding to individual providers.

Methodology Notes#

Contract values throughout this document represent estimates based on:

  1. State application budget narratives where available
  2. Comparable program allocations in other states
  3. Organization scope and capacity assessment
  4. Historical funding patterns for similar initiatives

Actual awards pending state procurement processes may differ significantly from estimates. This document will be updated as official award information becomes available.
